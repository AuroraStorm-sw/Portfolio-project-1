What yarn should I bring?


The world of yarn is vast and seemingly endless, and it comes down to what type of project you want to make. To narrow it down, here are the 3 most common types of yarn that you can look into.

  • Wool yarn:
    Wool is an excellent choice for practicing your crochet stitches. It is a resilient fiber and is forgiving of mistakes. It's commonly used to make warm wearables like scarves and hats, and also popular for cozy thick blankets.
  • Cotton yarn:
    Cotton yarn is popular to use when you want to make lighter clothes like bralettes, tunics, and cardigans as well as household items such as towels, dishcloths, market bags, coasters, and so on. It's commonly used when creating baby items because it is easy-care, soft, and available in vibrant colors.
  • Acrylic yarn:
    Overall, acrylic is a popular fiber with crochet enthusiasts; acrylic is widely available, it comes in a variety of colors, and it is usually one of the more affordable choices for yarn.
